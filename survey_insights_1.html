Strategic Takeaways · n=11

Validated Signals

  • Fit is the #1 failure point — 5 of 11 cite wrong fit as their biggest disappointment. Your clearest differentiation opportunity.
  • Athletic & modern is the dominant aesthetic (7/11). Clean, technical, sport-forward — not preppy polo, not fishing shirt.
  • Fabric feel ranks #1 across nearly all respondents. Sun protection ranks #4. Performance comes before protection in the buying decision.
  • Price-to-quality tension is real: 5/11 list it as an unmet need, 4/11 bought their last shirt on sale. Value perception will make or break launch pricing.
  • Purchase intent skews 4–6 out of 7 — genuine interest without wild enthusiasm. The right product story can close that gap.

Tensions & Risks

  • UV protection is your core proposition — but one outspoken respondent dismissed it as already adequate. Expect this objection in the market.
  • "Open to longer sleeves if it didn't look like a fishing shirt" appeared 3 times unprompted. The fishing shirt comparison is the brand's enemy. Design away from it deliberately.
  • Desert climates are entirely absent from this sample — your hypothesized best customer (hot/dry) isn't represented. Critical gap to close in round 2.
  • Versatility drives willingness to pay. If the shirt reads as golf-only, the price ceiling drops. Position lifestyle-first, performance-built.
  • Brand loyalty drove 3/11 purchases. As a new entrant, the product has to do the brand's job at launch — quality and design carry the full weight.
